## Project Narrative

**Project Summary**
TITANIC will be a large, surreal wooden sculpture of the sinking Titanic, broken in half, with the two halves forming a V. It will be climbable and explorable, with interior spaces inside the ship. The two smokestacks will shoot flames out of them.

**Physical Description**
Two sinking halves of the Titanic ship. The implication is that most of the ship is under water (under playa). The two halves of the ship will form a V-like amphitheater. The exterior of the ship will be minimalist and wood, with a colorful mural spilling out of both sides of the broken-open interior. The structure will be made of wood and will burn at the end of the event. The visual impact will be striking; we want people to be amazed at its scale when they ride up to it.

Exterior cladding will be minimalistic and made from upcycled materials. The interior rooms will all be different and interesting and not necessarily with a full unified aesthetic; we want to open them up to members of our greater community to take on and decorate and create interactivity for.

There will be a smaller LED sculpture between the two halves of the ship.

**Interactivity**
We hope people will climb into the sculpture and explore the rooms. We want the ship to serve as a large art piece that might host parties or the BRC philharmonic. By arranging the two halves of the ship at a slight angle, it forms a bit of an amphitheater. We hope that citizens will enjoy looking back at the city and watching a playa sunset from the vantage point of the top decks.

One of the things Burners love about Titanic's End is the opportunity to control the lighting on our MIDI control pads. We are going to create controls on the ship for people to interact with the LED sculpture sitting between the two halves.

**What is the philosophy of your piece?**
TITANIC represents the hubris of man and his faith in technology. As we continue to extract resources from the world at greater and greater scale, will we find a sustainable equilibrium with the planet? Or will the whole thing end in tragedy? We are running the experiment live.

Secondly, this piece represents the struggle and folly of building large playa art. The original Titanic was a monument to the ego of man that ended in disaster. Our TITANIC is a large-format sculpture that will be a gigantic lift to build, and will certainly come at immense human cost in blood, sweat, and tears, but ultimately will be worth it when it is enjoyed by the tens of thousands of citizens at BRC.

**Radical Inclusion, Diversity and Equity (R.I.D.E.)**
Titanic's End is built by a deliberately broad community of artists, engineers, fabricators, and volunteers spanning race, gender, age, class, and professional background. Our large-scale projects are structured to lower barriers to participation: skills are taught on-site, roles are not gatekept by credentials, and creative ownership of interior spaces is distributed to contributors rather than centralized. For TITANIC, we are opening interior rooms to collaborators from outside our core group, inviting underrepresented artists and first-time builders to shape distinct spaces within the structure. On playa, the work is intentionally legible, climbable, and socially porous—designed to foster gathering, shared perspective, and unprogrammed use rather than exclusionary or status-driven interaction.

## Safety and Engineering

**Safety and Engineering Strategies**
Safety will be addressed through a clear structural strategy and a controlled construction process. The structure is primarily gravity-based, organized around 4 main structural walls, with most elements working by their own weight. The roof and the chimney are treated as specific elements with independent structural calculations.

Public access is limited to safe areas. Steeply sloped surfaces are not accessible. The max accessible height is approximately 28', while the design ensures that potential fall heights are limited to no more than 5' through stepped geometry and controlled circulation.

The piece is designed for extreme wind. The structure will be complemented with ground anchors. The chimney will be reinforced with multiple tension cables, which are both structural and integral to the aesthetic language of the piece.

A licensed structural engineer and an experienced Build Lead will be involved to oversee engineering, construction sequencing, and on-site safety during installation.

**Build Materials**
The installation will be primarily constructed in wood, using dimensional lumber and engineered wood panels for structural walls, floors, and roof elements. Key structural components will be designed for gravity loads and complemented with ground anchors as required by playa conditions. Steel elements will be used selectively for connections, anchoring hardware, and tension cables, including those reinforcing the Titanic chimney. All materials are selected for durability, constructability, and safe deinstallation prior to burn.

## Environmental Sustainability

**Sustainability Efforts**
* Recycled / upcycled / locally sourced materials
* Artwork concept / educational message focused on sustainability
* Carbon offsets or similar mitigation strategies

**Leave No Trace Plan**
We will assign a dedicated Leave No Trace (LNT) Lead responsible for organizing daily clean-ups around TITANIC and overseeing the full LNT grid, including staging, work, and burn areas. The project team includes experienced architecture and build leads who bring deep experience executing LNT plans for large-scale wooden structures and burns. We will lay down protective decomposed granite (DG) pads beneath the sculpture and burn footprint to prevent heat damage and scarring of the playa. Post-burn, the crew will conduct multiple line sweeps using magnetic rakes, landscape rakes, and MOOP tools to remove all metal, ash, and debris. All MOOP will be packed out, and the site will be restored to BRC LNT standards in coordination with Art Support Services.

## Dimensions & Logistics

**Dimensions**

**Lighting Plan**
*Construction lighting:* We'll use portable light towers and 4' stand-mounted work lights, all IP-rated for outdoor use. Stand feet will be lagged into playa for wind stability. Power comes from diesel generators or industrial power packs with inverters. All sides will be lit to minimum 5 lux (federal highway pedestrian visibility standard).

*Completed installation:* Our piece is designed to draw people in at night, using uplight floods, LED rope lights, integrated artistic chandeliers, and perimeter key lights for plenty of illumination.

*Hazard mitigation:* Any guy lines will be illuminated with rope light or well-lit streamers. Ground-level hazards (protrusions, rebar) will be marked with illuminated, secured traffic cones. We have experience trenching power for safe cable management.

**Sound Elements**
There will be no sound generated by the piece itself. We envision TITANIC as a kind of amphitheater that could serve as seating at which other audio performances might occur.

**Post-Playa Modifications**
No modifications planned. The installation will be completely burned and will only live on in the memories of those who saw it on playa.
